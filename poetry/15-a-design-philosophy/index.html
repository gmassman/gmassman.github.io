A Design Philosophy

Posted at — Nov 14, 2020

I object to objects
 Terse as they may be
 Made of state and actions
 I'll claim subjectively
 Just a box of buttons
 Colored by their caller
 Bounded by opaqueness
 Bound for plans of squalor

Black is not a color
 Any caller truly knows
 Every object sees its context
 And its boundaries come to blows
 Once a moat was dug around
 A castle designed for a king
 Then additions were required
 And he was stuck inside his ring

Every change begets potential
 For what changes can be made
 And the structure of the program
 Guides which railways may be laid
 If the boundaries are narrow
 Then expansion causes fear
 But when boundaries can flex
 The path forward becomes clear

Ditch your objects and your classes
 There's no need for extra bindings
 Wisely name folders and files
 And avoid the late night grinding
 Get some sleep and dream of functions
 Keep the data structures light
 Build exactly what was asked for
 Test your code and make it tight!
